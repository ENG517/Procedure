# UNDERSTANDING WORDS IN CONTEXT
## Introduction
On most Hi-set tests there are many difficult vocabulary words, while learning new words during daily test preparation is suggested you will likely find new or unknown words on testing day. However unknown words do not need to result in confusion or an incorrect answer. Using a part of the text found in the mainidea proceedure, you will discover how to find the meaning of <i>contentious<i> even if you have not seen this word before. 

## Sample Paragraph
Before starting, let's look at the passage where we find the word:

The question of democracy remained <i>contentious<i>. While Japan established a constitution and parliament in 1889, real power remained concentrated in a small elite. Relations with the United States became increasingly strained, especially after the Immigration Act of 1924 banned Japanese immigration to America.

## STAGING

We find the word "contentious" in the third paragraph
The context involves Japan's struggle with democracy
The word appears at a turning point in the passage

## COACHING
Looking at the context, we see:

The sentence is followed by information about power being concentrated in a small elite
This happens despite having a constitution and parliament
The text shows two opposing views:

Support for democratic ideas
Desire to preserve traditions and old ways of doing things
Worsening relations with an important democratic country (United States) possibly impacting what Japanese think about democracy.


This conflict between different viewpoints helps us understand that "contentious" means disputed or controversial

## ALERTING

<b>Don't<b>: Just focus on the sentence containing "contentious"
<b>Do:</b> Notice how the entire passage shows ongoing debates and conflicts
<b>Remember:<b> The meaning becomes clearer when you see:

Pattern of disagreements about Japan's modernization
Tension between democracy and traditional power
Conflicts leading to bigger issues later
