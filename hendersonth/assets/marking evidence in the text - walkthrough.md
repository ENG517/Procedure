# How to Mark Evidence in Text

## Goal
<ul>
<li>Find and mark important evidence in any text</li>
<li>This is an important skill which will help you with tasks like finding the main idea, and words in context<li>
<li>Understand key points</li>
<li>Connect main ideas</li>
</ul>

## Materials Needed
<ul>
<li>Paper</li>
<li>Pen or pencil</li>
<li>Note paper for mapping ideas</li>
</ul>

## Step 1: First Reading
<ul>
<li>Read without marking</li>
<li>Get general idea</li>
<li>Notice paragraph structure</li>
</ul>

## Step 2: Second Reading with Marks
<ul>
<li>Circle key terms</li>
<li>Underline evidence</li>
<li>Draw arrows between connected ideas</li>
<li>Put brackets around main ideas</li>
</ul>

## Step 3: Make Margin Notes
<ul>
<li>Write "M" for main ideas</li>
<li>Write "E" for examples</li>
<li>Draw arrows to connect ideas</li>
<li>Add "?" for unclear points</li>
</ul>

## Step 4: Check Your Understanding
<ul>
<li>Can you explain main points?</li>
<li>Did you find key evidence?</li>
<li>Are ideas connected?</li>
<li>Does everything marked serve a purpose?</li>
</ul>

## Tips for Success
<ul>
<li>Don't mark everything</li>
<li>Use consistent marking system</li>
<li>Review your marks for meaning</li>
<li>Make sure you can explain why each mark is important</li>
</ul>

> Remember: Your marks should help you understand the text better!
