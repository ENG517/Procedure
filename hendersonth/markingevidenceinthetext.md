# How to Mark Evidence in Text
## Goal
* Find and mark important evidence in any text
* This is an important skill which will help you with tasks like finding the main idea, and words in context
* Understand key points  
* Connect main ideas

## Materials Needed
* Paper
* Pen or pencil 
* Note paper for mapping ideas

## Step 1: First Reading
* Read without marking
* Get general idea (if you are not sure how to do this do not worry, please refer to the [Main Idea Guide](mainideaproceedure.md)
* Notice paragraph structure

## Step 2: Second Reading with Marks
* Circle words that you find important to the passage
### But Which words are important? If you are unsure please look at the section below.

 Identifying Important Words in Non-Fiction and Social Studies Texts
#### Core Principles
 Look for words that signal:
  * Key events and dates
  * Major changes or transitions
  * Significant people or places
  * Cause and effect relationships
  * Themes and concepts

#### 1. Identify Time Markers
* Circle dates and temporal phrases
* Examples from text:
 * "1848" - marks starting point
 * "1853" - Perry's arrival
 * "1868" - Boshin War
 * "1905" - Victory over Russia

#### 2. Mark Key Historical Figures & Places
* Circle names of:
 * Important people
 * Significant locations
* Examples from text:
 * "Commodore Matthew Perry"
 * "Emperor Meiji"
 * "Japan" and "United States"

#### 3. Highlight Transition Words
* Look for words indicating:
 * Change
 * Consequence
 * Contrast
* Examples from text:
 * "triggered"
 * "became"
 * "however"
 * "despite"

#### 4. Circle Concept Words
* Identify words that:
 * Define major ideas
 * Represent themes
* Examples from text:
 * "modernization"
 * "industrialization"
 * "democracy"
 * "imperial"

#### 5. Mark Action Words
* Focus on verbs that:
 * Show significant actions
 * Indicate major changes
* Examples from text:
 * "acquired"
 * "abolished"
 * "defeated"
 * "implemented"
  
## Contiune to mark selectively
* Underline evidence
* Draw arrows between connected ideas
* Put brackets around main ideas

## Step 3: Make Margin Notes
* Write "M" for main ideas
* Write "E" for examples
* Draw arrows to connect ideas
* Add "?" for unclear points

## Step 4: Check Your Understanding
* Can you explain main points?
* Did you find key evidence?
* Are ideas connected?
* Does everything marked serve a purpose?

## Tips for Success
* Don't mark everything
* Use consistent marking system
* Review your marks for meaning
* Make sure you can explain why each mark is important

> Remember: Your marks should help you understand the text better!
