## How do your procedures address the needs of a "reading to learn to do audience"? Provide some specific examples that connect back to Redish's noted features of such an audience.

My procedures offer a unique take on the "reading to learn to do" audience because they act as examples that can be applied to all WordPress websites in the context of updating the Technical Communication Association's website. The reader learns the importance of the task they're doing for TCA while gaining transferable WordPress skills through accomplishing their task. The reader is learning while doing (completing my coaching steps).

## How do your procedures follow the WTGA Staging, Coaching, and Alerting stylistic conventions? Provide some specific examples that connect back to the criteria from Hart-Davidson.

In each procedure, my staging steps are located at the beginning of the document in the “About” and “Prerequisites” sections. Staging moves state the goal of the task, overall purpose (to orient the reader), and prerequisites on what the reader will need to complete the task successfully. My “About” and “Prerequisites” categories address those conventions. My “How To” sections house my coaching moves. Each phrase (except for rare circumstances) starts with an imperative verb and contains text and images that detail how the step should be completed and the desired outcome. My Alerting moves are labeled as “Note” that give readers additional information and advice on how to avoid problems.

## How do your procedures follow your task orientation work? In other words, based on your audience and their goals, discuss how you oriented your SCA moves to the tasks.

My procedures were task-focused (emphasis on coaching moves), with my staging and alerting moves acting as supplementary material for the tasks. I oriented my moves to how my audience (the TCA leadership board) would look for and complete tasks. I started with my staging moves, a brief overview of what the documentation aims to accomplish, and the materials needed to perform said tasks. Then, I focused on coaching moves, adding alerting moves after steps necessary for additional context guidance. I didn’t reinvent the wheel for my audience - I followed traditional procedure structuring conventions. The goal of each move was for the audience member to accomplish their intended tasks easily. Since the audience is assumed to be familiar with technical communication but new to WordPress, I ensured the copy was easily accessible but not unnecessarily detailed. The steps for the “Updating a Menu” procedure were more condensed because, though a beginner audience, a lot of approval has to take place within the organization for the edits to be made, meaning the person making them would most likely have some familiarity or concrete plan.
