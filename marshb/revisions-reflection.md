### 2. [How to Create a Podcast Vocal EQ](marsh-revisions-how-to-use-EQ-T2.md)

My second procedure was created because I noticed that my intended user may want more information about how to properly use an EQ (a very important part of any vocal chain). Instead of display the many uses of an EQ and seeking to insight transfer of the material in my user, I simply instructed them in a step-by-step manner how to create a basic EQ for podcasts. - I used alerting moves as extra information that is relevant to the user in case they encounter challenges creating their vocal chain or working with the EQ. This information is not integral to accomplishing the procedures task, but it is useful for users who experience the problems mentioned in the alerting moves. 
- In the EQ procedure, I used alerting moves as considerations for users if they have higher voices, accidently boost or cut a frequency too much, or wish to enhanche the frequency boost or cut they have created. This information is not integral to accomplishing the procedures task, but it is useful for users who wish to morph their EQ more specifically to their personal voice or style. 

#### Considerations Section

- I created a considerations section for my EQ procedure because it is good for users to know that EQ's are incredibly personalized effects. What works best for one person will not work for another. This is a basic EQ that will work for most voices, but there may need to be tweaks to best suit the EQ outline to a specific type of voice
