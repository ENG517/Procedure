# How to use an Equalizer in a Podcast Vocal Chain

#### Learn what frequencies to cut and boost so that your podcast vocal has a clear, natural, and warm sound.

## Prerequisites
#### Before you begin, you need to do this.
- Create a vocal track.
- Place a Channel EQ within your vocal chain.

## Setting up an EQ for a Clear Podcast Vocal

### 1. Remove Low End Frequencies
- Remove frequencies below **80-100 Hz** to eleminate any unnecessary bass. 
- For higher-pitched voices, you might even set it as high as **120 Hz**. 

![80 to 100](<assets/images/revision screenshots/t2-80to100.jpg>)

> *Avoid going higher than **120 Hz** to prevent thinning out the voice.*

### 2. Remove *Muddy* Frequencies
- Cut frequencies between the **200-400 Hz range** to remove muddiness, which can make the voice sound boxy.

![200 to 400](<assets/images/revision screenshots/t2-200to400.jpg>)

- Use a narrow Q (around 1-1.5) and try cutting around 250-350 Hz by 1-3 dB.

### 3. Boost Warm Bass Frequencies
- Boost slightly in the **100-200 Hz range** if the voice sounds too thin or lacks warmth.

![100 to 200](<assets/images/revision screenshots/t2-100to300.jpg>)

>*A small boost of 1-2 dB in this area can add fullness, especially for **deeper voices**. For higher voices, this might not be necessary.*

### 4. Add Clarity and Presence in the middle frequencies
- Boost in the **2-5 kHz range** to add clarity and presence, which helps the voice cut through and stay intelligible.

![2K to 5K](<assets/images/revision screenshots/t2-5Kto8K.jpg>)

### 5. Reduce Harshness and Sibilance in the Higher Frequencies
- Reduce slightly around **5-8 kHz** to reduce sibilance if “S” and “T” sounds are harsh.

![5K to 8K](<assets/images/revision screenshots/t2-5Kto8K.jpg>)

> *A gentle reduction (1-2 dB) here can help tame harsh sounds without dulling the voice. You can also follow up with a De-Esser if necessary.*

6. Enhance Brightness in the Higher Frequencies
- Add a subtle boost in the 10-12 kHz range for air and brightness if the voice sounds too dull.

![10K to 12K](<assets/images/revision screenshots/t2-10Kto12K.jpg>)

> *Keep it subtle (around 1-2 dB) to avoid making the voice sound unnatural.*

### 7. Check Your End Result
- Your EQ should look like this:

![End Result: EQ](<assets/images/revision screenshots/t2-EQ.jpg>)

## Considerations
### Adjust and Listen
- Fine-tune each band based on your voice and room acoustics. Make small adjustments and listen back on headphones and speakers to ensure the voice sounds clear, warm, and balanced.
- Check that each frequency range complements your natural tone, without creating harsh or boomy qualities.
