My intended audiences in both cases are beginner level users and therefore, keeping the information clear and the steps precise was important. 
There is minimal explanation of the science or theory behind the actions. 
In the case of the pavlova recipe, there were notes at the bottom that provided slightly more context to particular steps but these notes were there for those interested and not required to complete the task. 

![alt text](https://github.com/ENG517/Procedure/blob/964cccd68e62139e66286a11e3318c7ea31a9f69/youngm/assets/images/screenshots/Pavlova%20Procedure%20Alerting.png)

The notes also functioned as “alerting” in the context of this procedure, because they provide an explanation as to why certain steps help avoid a failure state which could include having a dissatisfactory final product or losing motivation to complete the procedure. 
The “framing” element of the recipe is present at the beginning, by informing the reader of the goals of the procedure. 

![alt text](https://github.com/ENG517/Procedure/blob/964cccd68e62139e66286a11e3318c7ea31a9f69/youngm/assets/images/screenshots/Pavlova%20Procedure%20Framing.png)

The visual elements are there to assist a reader in understanding what the final product is supposed to be.

The “coaching” element of the recipe is the main body of the procedure which provides the ingredients and the steps. 
The visual elements here help assist the reader fill in any contextual gaps, because not everyone is going to know what “stiff peaks” are. 

![alt text](https://github.com/ENG517/Procedure/blob/964cccd68e62139e66286a11e3318c7ea31a9f69/youngm/assets/images/screenshots/Pavlova%20Procedure%20Coaching.png)

For the procedure on how to write a cover letter, the framing elements are the beginning of the instructions and lay out the goal and purpose of the product. 

![alt text](https://github.com/ENG517/Procedure/blob/964cccd68e62139e66286a11e3318c7ea31a9f69/youngm/assets/images/screenshots/Cover%20letter%20procedure%20framing.png)

The “coaching” elements are short but clear and concise.

![alt text](https://github.com/ENG517/Procedure/blob/964cccd68e62139e66286a11e3318c7ea31a9f69/youngm/assets/images/screenshots/cover%20letter%20procedure%20coaching%20.png)

Quite frankly, I am not sure this was the best topic to choose for this particular project because writing a cover letter ended up being a more fluid process than I was initially expecting.
The tips section of this procedure function as the “alerting” element by reminding the reader of logistical parameters.

![alt text](https://github.com/ENG517/Procedure/blob/964cccd68e62139e66286a11e3318c7ea31a9f69/youngm/assets/images/screenshots/cover%20letter%20procedure%20alerting.png)

The recipe also was a procedure that focused on producing a successful (edible) product and covered the whole process from start to finish because baking a pavlova is a scientifically testable process. 
Whereas the procedure on how to write a cover letter was more of an outline of the process, because the content will be different and unique for each user.
Because the audience for both procedures were beginners there was basic terminology used rather than advanced terminology.
Additionally, certain information was withheld, specifically in the pavlova recipe to avoid the recipe from being intimidating or deemed too complicated by the intended audience. 
The decision was made to not include visuals in the cover letter procedure because the mock-ups and examples that were readily available would have been the work of other people. However, part of reflection is understanding where improvements and changes could have been made.
If given the opportunity in the next project to expand on this procedure, mock-ups and examples created specifically for this class will undoubtably be included.
