# How to Make Muhammara

Mumhammra is a smokey and sweet dip made primarily of roasted peppers. Similar in texture to it’s more common cousin - hummus. This spread combines walnuts, tahini, red bell pepper, chiles, and pomegranate molasses. The recipe originated in Syria but there are regional variations of the recipe across the Middle East.

## Equipment
- Stove 
- Oven
- Sheet pan
- Food processor
- Measuring cups

## Ingredients
- 1 cup walnuts
- 3 large red bell peppers
- 2 Tbsp. olive oil
- 2 Tbsp. your favorite type of mild chile flakes
- 1 Tbsp. tahini
- 1 tsp. lemon juice
- ½ tsp. paprika
- 2 Tbsp. pomegranate molasses
- salt to task
- ½ cup fine breadcrumbs (optional)

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 350°. Toast walnuts on a baking sheet, about 8–10 minutes and then set aside.
2. Turn the oven up to 425 degrees. Roast the bell peppers on a baking sheet, turning occasionally, 12–15 minutes. Then switch the oven to broil and keep an eye on the peppers as their skins start to char and blister. This will take about 5-8 minutes depending on the oven.

![alt text](youngm\assets\images\screenshots\Roastingpeppers.jpg)

3. Remove as much skin as you can from the bell peppers; remove and discard the ribs (the white fleshy parts) and seeds.
4. Pulse the bell peppers, oil, chile flakes, tahini, lemon juice, paprika, toasted walnuts, and 2 Tbsp. pomegranate molasses in a food processor until mostly smooth; season muhammara with salt.
5. If the texture of the dip is too wet or too smooth, add anywhere from a dash to a ½ cup of breadcrumbs
6. Transfer muhammara to a small bowl; drizzle with more pomegranate molasses and top with any leftover chopped walnuts.

![alt text]()

Serve with a crusty bread, pita, or naan
