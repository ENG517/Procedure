# Washing Laundry with NC State Laundry Machines

University Housing offers all residents living on-campus access to free laundry facilities. Please be mindful of other residents when doing your laundry in our common spaces.

Residents must use their own detergents, dryer sheets, etc.

## Washing your Clothes

1. Load your dirty clothes into the washing machine.
   * **Optional**: separate clothes into whites and colors.
  
2. Add detergent to the washer.
   * If using **detergent pods**, place the pod directly in the washing machine drum.
   * If using **liquid detergent**, pour detergent into the detergent compartment in the drawer in the top right. 
  ![Front view of washing machine, detergent drawer is highlighted](assets/images/washing-machine-front.png)

3. **Optional**: add bleach and fabric softener to the drawer.
   
4. Close door firmly.
   
5. Configure the settings with the control panel.
   * **Note**: settings will revert to default after each cycle.
  
6. Press start.
  
Your laundry is now going through the wash cycle. The control panel will display the remaining in the cycle. 

**Tip**: Set a timer on your phone to keep track of your laundry and remove your laundry promptly.

## Drying your Clothes

1. Load wet clothes into the dryer drum.
   * **Alert**: Check the lint trap to ensure it is clean.
   * **Optional**: Add a dryer sheet or dryer balls to reduce dry time.

2. Close the dryer door.
   
3. Select the desired mode with the correct control panel.
   * ex. If you are using the bottom dryer, use the right control panel.
   ![Stackable dryers from the front, the control panel is highlighted](assets/images/stackdryer-front.png)

4. Press start

Your clothes are now drying. The time remaining on the dry cycle will be displayed on the control panel.

**Tip**: Set a time on your phone to remember to remove your clothes from the machine.
